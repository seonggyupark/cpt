II.1.5) Short description of the contract or purchase(s):

The GNSS Regulation sets forth that the Galileo FOC procurement is split into a set of 6 main work packages (1. System engineering support, 2. Ground mission infrastructure completion, 3. Ground control infrastructure completion, 4. Satellites, 5. Launchers and 6. Operations). Each work package constitutes a specific lot of the Galileo FOC procurement without giving rise to multiple simultaneous procurement strands for individual work packages. The content of the 6 work packages in which the Galileo FOC is organised is described in the tender information pack which can be downloaded at the following address: http://emits.esa.int (News) and is to be considered as an integral part of the present contract notice.

Information about lots

Lot No 1

Title: System support
1) Short description:
The system activities address the procurement of the industrial system engineering technical assistance (SETA) tasks for the Galileo full operational capability in support to ESA in its role of overall system prime responsible.
These activities include system engineering and system assembly integration and verification, including security, along with development of the associated tools and validation facilities and test receivers (test user segment).
The system activities shall be undertaken by an industrial consortium with demonstrated background and experience in the key technical disciplines, under the leadership of an industrial prime contractor.
2) Common procurement vocabulary (CPV):
35320000.
3) Quantity or scope:
EUR 120 000 000. Indexation has already been factored into this estimation.

Lot No 2

Title: Ground mission segment
1) Short description:
The GMS contractor will be responsible for the upgrade of the design and development carried out in the frame of the Galileo development phase ('In Orbit Validation' ? IOV) as necessary to meet FOC requirements.
This implies the procurement and deployment of additional facilities complementing the initial IOV configuration (up to 3 Galileo control centres, up to 30 to 40 Galileo sensor stations and up to 9 uplink stations are required for the Galileo full operational capability).
The GMS contractor will also be responsible for the overall segment integration, verification and qualification in order to meet the functional and performances requirements allocated to the GMS. Such activities will take due account of the gradual build-up of Galileo services provision.
2) Common procurement vocabulary (CPV):
35320000.
3) Quantity or scope:
EUR 270 000 000. Indexation has already been factored into this estimation.
5) Additional information about lots:

Additional information about the 'Ground mission segment' lot is contained in the tender information package which can be downloaded at the following address: http://emits.esa.int (news).

Lot No 3

Title: Ground control segment
1) Short description:
The GCS contractor will be responsible for the upgrade of the design and development carried out in the frame of the Galileo development phase ('In Orbit Validation' ? IOV) as necessary to meet FOC requirements.
This implies the procurement and deployment of additional facilities complementing the initial IOV configuration (up to 3 Galileo control centres, up to 5 telemetry, tracking and command facilities are required for the Galileo full operational capability).
The GCS contractor will also be responsible for the overall segment integration, verification and qualification in order to meet the functional and performances requirements allocated to the GCS. Such activities will take due account of the result of operational experience with the IOV satellites.

Lot No 4

Title: Space segment
1) Short description:
This lot concerns the procurement and deployment of 26 satellites in orbit and the procurement of a set of 2 to 4 'on-ground' spares (for risk mitigation). The 26 satellites will complement the existing 4 satellites produced and deployed in the development phase.
The tasks related to this activity will encompass the responsibility for developing, manufacturing and testing the procured Galileo satellites. This includes management, engineering, project control and product assurance, design, development, assembly, integration and test of the satellites, regular updating and upgrade of the satellite design taking into account production and in-orbit experience, transportation of the satellite, propellant and any relevant support equipment, plus personnel to/from the launch site, performance of the launch campaign, commissioning and in-orbit testing of the satellites, satellite maintenance in support to nominal operations, production of any other necessary hardware, software and documentation.
It shall also encompass overall space segment security activities, including supporting activities leading to accreditation of the Galileo overall system, and any necessary security measures for protection of products, documentation, infrastructures and personnel, including security measures to be implemented at the launch site.
2) Common procurement vocabulary (CPV):
35320000.
3) Quantity or scope:
EUR 840 000 000. Indexation has already been factored into this estimation.

Lot No 5

Title: Launchers
1) Short description:
This lot concerns the procurement of the launch services for the Galileo full operational capability (FOC) phase. The launch services are intended for the deployment of 26 Galileo satellites in medium Earth orbit. The satellites shall be placed by the launcher in the Galileo circular orbit, with a semi-major axis of 29 600 km. The mass of each satellite is approximately 730 kg.
Responsibility under this lot shall encompass overall management and engineering, including project control and product assurance, production of the launcher vehicles, design, development, test and production of dedicated fully-qualified dispensers/adaptors for the Galileo satellites, which shall include the hold-down and release system, procurement of satellites dummies for dispensers/adaptors qualification if needed, conducting of all launcher/satellite combined operations, including integration of the satellites on the launchers, supply of standard launch campaign facilities compliant with the satellite launch campaign requirements, launch operations.
It shall also encompass all necessary security measures for enabling the 'Space segment' contractor to exercise the necessary protection of products, documentation, infrastructures and personnel, as well as the necessary security measures for recovery of debris following a launcher failure.
2) Common procurement vocabulary (CPV):
35320000.
3) Quantity or scope:
EUR 700 000 000. Indexation has already been factored into this estimation.
5) Additional information about lots:

Additional information about the 'Launchers' lot is contained in the tender information package which can be downloaded at the following address: http://emits.esa.int (news).

Lot No 6

Title: Operations
1) Short description:
The operations services for the Galileo full operations capability phase comprise operations of all deployed spacecrafts in the Galileo constellation, and the operations of the ground control and ground mission segments facilities, both in the Galileo control centres and in the remote sites, including first-line maintenance of all ground assets and provision of the relevant integrated logistic services.
These services include the preparation of all associated operations products (flight and ground operations procedures, maintenance plans etc.) as well as participation in the system testing and operational validation activities.
Monitoring and control of the overall security configuration of the whole Galileo system, including security operations, network security, and site security is part of the operations services activities.
The operations services include the procurement, first-line maintenance, and operations of the communication infrastructure between the GCCs and the remote sites, Galileo service providers and user services.
2) Common procurement vocabulary (CPV):
35320000.
3) Quantity or scope:
EUR 170 000 000. Indexation has already been factored into this estimation.
